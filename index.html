The giant panda is a bear species endemic to China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body. The name "giant panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda, a neighboring musteloid.

Giant pandas help to keep their mountain forests healthy by spreading seeds in their droppings, which helps vegetation to thrive. The Giant panda's forested habitat is also important for local people for food, income and fuel for cooking and heating.

Pandas are native to China and their origin dates back millions of years. They are part of the bear family and were once classified as raccoons. Pandas were first described by a French naturalist in 1869 and have since become an endangered species due to habitat loss and other human factors.
